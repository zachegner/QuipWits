What is This?

QuipWits is a party game for people who think they're funnier than everyone else. Spoiler: only one of you is right.

You'll get weird prompts, write ridiculous answers, and vote on which response deserves the crown. It's basically a wit showdown wrapped in synthwave aesthetics.

One person hosts the game on a big screen (a laptop, TV, or anything with a browser). Everyone else joins on their phones. No app download needed. Just scan, type, and start roasting.

Minimum 3
Maximum 10

Getting In

  1. Find the Host Screen Look for the TV or laptop showing a big glowing room code. That's your portal.
  2. Scan or Type Grab your phone. Either scan the QR code on screen or type the URL into your browser. Then punch in that four-letter room code.
  3. Pick a Name Choose something memorable. Or embarrassing. Your call. Everyone will see it when you win (or lose spectacularly).
  4. Wait for Launch Hang tight until the host hits that big START button. Maybe stretch your typing fingers.

Pro Tip

Stay on the same WiFi network as the host. QuipWits runs locally, so no internet dependency once you're connected. Just don't wander off to the other side of the house.

The Breakdown

The game runs through two regular rounds plus The Last Wit finale. Each round has three phases:

Answer

You get prompts. You write something clever, dumb, or both.

Vote

Two answers face off. Pick your favorite. Democracy at its finest.

Score

Points get tallied. Egos get bruised. Repeat.

Round Mechanics

The Prompts

Each player gets two prompts per round. You'll see fill-in-the-blank questions or open-ended setups. The goal? Write something that makes people pick yours over the competition.

"The worst thing to say on a first date is ___"

"Scientists just discovered a new planet called ___"

You've got 90 seconds per prompt. The clock is real. If time runs out before you submit, you get "[No answer]" and zero dignity.

The Showdown

Every prompt gets answered by two players. During voting, both answers appear head-to-head on the big screen. Nobody knows who wrote what until the reveal.

Everyone except the two authors votes. No voting for yourself (we thought of that).

Watch Out

If you and your opponent write the exact same answer, that's a JINX. No points for either of you. Great minds think alike, but only one can win.

Points

This isn't participation trophy territory. Points matter. Here's how they stack up:

What Happened Points
Each vote your answer gets +100
QUIPWIT - Everyone voted for you +100 Bonus
JINX - Same answer as opponent 0

A QuipWit is the holy grail. It means every single voter chose your answer. You get the standard vote points PLUS a 100-point bonus for being unanimously hilarious.

The Last Wit

After two rounds, it's finale time. One final prompt. Everyone answers. The stakes are ridiculous.

Each game randomly selects one of three challenge modes:

Flashback Lash

Complete a story setup like "I was at my wedding when suddenly..."

Word Lash

Create a phrase from starting letters like "T. F. N." (Totally Fake News)

Acro Lash

Expand an acronym (3-5 letters) like "L. O. L." (Llamas On Ladders)

Instead of head-to-head voting, all answers are shown at once (anonymously). Each player picks their favorite answer.

What Happened Points
Each vote your answer gets +100
Winner bonus +300

This is where comebacks happen. Or where the leader extends their reign. Either way, it's going to get loud.

Word/Acro Tips

For letter-based modes, make sure each word starts with the right letter. The game checks (gently) but creativity counts more than perfection.

Winning Tactics

Know Your Audience

Inside jokes land harder than generic humor. If your group spent last summer obsessing over that one meme, use it. Context is comedy.

Short Hits Different

You've got 100 characters. Sometimes "Jeff" is funnier than a three-sentence explanation. Brevity is the soul of wit, or whatever that guy said.

The Unexpected Angle

Everyone's first instinct is probably the same. Your second or third idea? That's where gold lives. Zig when they zag.

Commit to the Bit

Half-clever answers die in voting. If you're going absurd, go ALL the way absurd. Confidence reads through text.

The Real Secret

Make yourself laugh first. If you're grinning while typing it, you're probably onto something.

Themed Rounds

The host can set a theme before starting. When AI prompts are enabled, this generates custom questions around that topic.

Themes like "80s movies," "office life," "dating disasters," or "space exploration" shape what kind of prompts appear. It's the same game, but with focused chaos.

No theme? Standard prompts pull from a curated collection of weird questions designed to get strange answers.

Practical Stuff

Connection Dropped?

Refresh your browser and rejoin with the same name. The game tracks players, so you'll jump back in where you left off.

Timer Running Down?

Submit early if you're done. Better to lock in a decent answer than panic-type something forgettable in the last five seconds.

Game Paused?

The host can pause anytime. Bathroom breaks, snack runs, whatever. Your phone will show a pause screen. Just wait it out.

Want to Quit?

Close the browser tab. The game continues without you. Your prompts either get auto-filled or skipped. No hard feelings (from the game, at least).
